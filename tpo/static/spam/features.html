Mark Twain once wrote : "Benaras is older than history, older than tradition, older even than legend and looks twice as old as all of them put together".
Varanasi, or Benaras, (also known as Kashi) is one of the oldest living cities in the world. It gets it name from the Varuna and Assi rivers which are believed to have originated from here. The city is one of the most visited tourist destinations in India. To every visitor; Varanasi offers a breathtaking experience. The rays of the dawn shimmering across the Ganges, the high-banks, the temples and shrines along the banks bathed in a golden hue soul stirring hymns and mantras alongwith the fragrance of incense filling the air and the refreshing dip in the holy waters gently splashing at the Ghats.
Varanasi is the microcosm of Hinduism. A city of traditional classical culture, glorified by myth and legend and sanctified by religion, it has always attracted a large number of pilgrims and worshippers from time immemorial. To be in Varanasi is an experience in itself - the discovery of the eternal oneness of the body and soul.
Varanasi is also renowned for its rich tapestry of music, arts, crafts and education. Some of the world's renowned exponents India has produced in these fields were schooled in Varanasi's cultural ethos. The Banaras Hindu University (BHU) is the largest residential university of Asia and has been a source of quality education for more than a century.

It is not a city you just see. It is a city you feel.
